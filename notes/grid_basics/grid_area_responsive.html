Using Grid Area templates allows for really simple rearrangement of content at different breakpoints, making the layout responsive very easily.

In this example, at the mobile breakpoint we just let the blocks stack like blocks, one after another. No CSS Grid needed for this.

Then at tablet widths, we merge the menu and the header, rearranging their order on the page without changing their order in the HTML, and putting them side-by-side.

Finally, at desktop widths, we put the menu next to the content, and the header and footer span the whole width.
